Why the Universal Health system?

Because Healthcare is a Human Right!

The U.S. ranks last on access to care, administrative efficiency, equity, and health care outcomes, but second on measures of care process. 1 in 10 people in the U.S. don't have access to healthcare. In fact, deaths from lack of health insurance now exceed those caused by many other common causes of death... such as kidney disease. In 2019 a Gallup poll found that 25% of Americans said they or a family member have delayed medical treatment for a serious illness due to the costs of medical care. An additional 8% reported delaying medical treatment for less serious illnesses. In 2019, 73.7% of uninsured adults stated that they were uninsured because the cost of coverage was too high.

Thanks to the ACA, in 2016, the amount of Americans decreased by nearly 20 million! Yet to this day, 12 states still refuse to expand Medicaid. No matter how much it would help their citizens. These states include: WY, SD, KS, TX, WI, TN, MS, AL, GA, FL, NC, & SC. All of which, unsurprisingly, are Republican led states. During Trumps Presidency, the number of uninsured Americans jumped by 23 million, which led to thousands more deaths. Lack of insurance leads to a 40% greater risk of death in this country. A rather extreme increase from 1993.

Lack of Healthcare

Your mental state can be negitively affected by overly expensive healthcare, or lack thereof. Leading to even more problems in ones life... like job loss & poverty. Which only exacerbates the healthcare aspect. Much like being incapable of purchasing a new matress before your old matress causes long term back problems. Or being capable of buying a filling for a cavity, can lead to either losing the tooth, or even worse... an abcess. I personally know a family who lost a loved one as a result of an abcess he was too poor to afford to fix. Simply put, lack of healthcare impedes a persons ability to recieve the care they need when, they need it!!!
