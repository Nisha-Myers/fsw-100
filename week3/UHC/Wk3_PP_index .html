~About the Author~

Why the Universal Health system?

Because Healthcare is a Human Right!

The U.S. ranks last on access to care, administrative efficiency, equity, and health care outcomes, but second on measures of care process. 1 in 10 people in the U.S. don't have access to healthcare. In fact, deaths from lack of health insurance now exceed those caused by many other common causes of death... such as kidney disease. In 2019 a Gallup poll found that 25% of Americans said they or a family member have delayed medical treatment for a serious illness due to the costs of medical care. An additional 8% reported delaying medical treatment for less serious illnesses. In 2019, 73.7% of uninsured adults stated that they were uninsured because the cost of coverage was too high. Thanks to the ACA, in 2016, the amount of Americans decreased by nearly 20 million! Yet to this day, 12 states still refuse to expand Medicaid. No matter how much it would help their citizens. These states include: WY, SD, KS, TX, WI, TN, MS, AL, GA, FL, NC, & SC. All of which, unsurprisingly, are Republican led states. During Trumps Presidency, the number of uninsured Americans jumped by 23 million, which led to thousands more deaths. Lack of insurance leads to a 40% greater risk of death in this country... a rather extreme increase from 1993.

Medicaid, the closest this country has come to Universal Healthcare... While with Universal healthcare, or (UHC) every. single. citizen. is provided with quality healthcare regardless of ones ability to pay or what state ones lucky enough to live in. The care is made possible by everyones contribution, by paying a little higher taxes than we do here. Which, most people, in most countries, are more than happy to pay if it helps their fellow citizens. Sadly, we don't live in a country like that, in fact, we're probably the most selfish of all "advanced" countries on this planet in my humble opinion. We unfortunately live in a country where a person in need is spat at & told "I'm not paying for YOUR healthcare! That's MY money! You could die for all I care!!!". Something I can unfortunately attest to, as I've spent a lot of time on political sites... It breaks my heart every time I see something like that. But I digress... The UHC model allows for even a homeless individual to be provided with much needed healthcare. Which sometimes is precisely what they need to pull themselves out of the streets. The majority of progressive countries have incorporated UHC... These coutries include: Austria, Belarus, Bulgaria, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Iceland, Isle of Man, Italy, Luxembourg, Malta, Moldova, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Russia, Serbia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Ukraine, and the United Kingdom.

The incorporation of the Universal Health system has substantially increased their estimated lifespan. The U.S. rates 29th in the world for citizen life expectancy. While citizens in countries with UHC tend to live 80+ years! The U.S is the ONLY wealthy, industrialized nation IN THE WORLD without Universal Health Care & it's an absolute disgrace!

Lack of Healthcare

Your mental state can be negitively affected by overly expensive healthcare, or lack thereof. Leading to even more problems in ones life... like job loss & poverty. Which only exacerbates the healthcare aspect. Much like being incapable of purchasing a new matress before your old matress causes long term back problems. Or being capable of buying a filling for a cavity, can lead to either losing the tooth, or even worse... an abcess. I personally know a family who lost a loved one as a result of an abcess he was too poor to afford to fix. Simply put, lack of healthcare impedes a persons ability to recieve the care they need when, they need it!!!

In Summation...

Everyone deserves access to proper healthcare. We shouldn't have to break ourselves to be relieved of pain. Whether it be physical or mental. The cost of living is far too high as it is. We have to pay our taxes, pay for water, pay for heat, pay for AC, pay for food, pay for gas, pay for our transportation constantly even if we've already bought it (the same goes for housing & our phones), pay for school, pay for clothes, pay for hygenic products to stay clean & socially acceptable, pay for internet, & more...
